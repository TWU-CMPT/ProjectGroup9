Introduction

Breathify aims to offer those who suffer from anxiety or stress a way to minimize their symptoms using a variety of breathing exercises. Breathing exercises have been shown to be an effective way of combating moments of high anxiety or high stress.

 

The application will provide two main methods of performing breathing exercises. One method will present a countdown timer for different stages of the selected breathing exercise accompanied by additional aural and visual cues. Visual cues will be used to instruct the user on how to perform the breathing exercise. The aural cues will also instruct the user on how to perform the breathing exercise allowing for the exercise to be done without having to read the visual cues. These aural cues have the option of being muted.

 

The second method is an interactive game where breathing exercise steps are to be followed more strictly to encourage more focus with points awarded for closely following the exercise. The game works by having the user breathe into the microphone on the phone or on a peripheral which will cause a pin to move along a music chart-like display vertically. The selected breathing exercise will be translated to a line with segments which slope up, slope down, or stay flat depending on whether the user must breathe out, breathe in, or hold their breath, respectively. The length of these segments will vary depending on the breathing exercise. This line will scroll along the music chart towards the pin. The user will have to make the pin follow the line by breathing into the microphone.

 

The user will have a variety of breathing exercises to choose from including those that only require simple breathing to those that require a physical activity to them. These exercises will be presented in a list which can be sorted and filtered by categories and keywords. The breathing exercises will be tagged with multiple keywords and sorted into different categories.

 

For some of the breathing exercises, users will have the option to track their heart rate during the exercise using the mobile phones camera. This data will be presented to the user in a section which shows the user their usage history. This usage history will consist of entries of the exercise done, when the exercise was done, heart rate information, and a user rating of the exercise.

 

This heart rate data along with data about the overall usage of the application will be used to inform the user about the effects of the breathing exercises and their activity pattern related to the application.

 

There will be a section where users can read more about the positive effects of breathing as well as extra resources for seeking aid for their anxiety or stress concerns.

 

This application seeks to be an effective tool for reducing anxiety and stress as well as being a good resource for those looking for aid in dealing with anxiety or stress.

 

Intended Audience List


Users

The users are the people that have downloaded our app from the App Store. Users are not interested in the technical bits and pieces of the app and have little technical experience or expertise. They want to use Breathify to de-stress and to track breathing patterns and heart rate.

 

Developers

Developers will be the members of our team, and we have both the technical experience and expertise, from previous projects, to do our job. One key skill they will have to learn before beginning to develop Breathify is the language Swift. Also, the developers do not know the needs of the target audience as well as the sponsors. Therefore, they will be asking for guidance throughout the project. The goal for developers is to create an app that contains the features as detailed below.


Testers

The testers will be members of the team and potentially some testers outside of the team. The team has experience and expertise in testing.  The testers goal will be to develop validation tests in order to find potential bugs and report them to developers. This will ensure that the app works as planned.


Sponsors

The sponsors for this project are the SFU Health Promotion Team, specifically Dylan Leroy and Rosie Dhaliwal. They have little technical experience and expertise, but they know the needs of the intended users best. The developers will be looking to them for feedback throughout the life of the development stage. Their goal is to see positive experiences from users of Breathify.


Database Administrator

Maintaining the database will be the responsibility of all members of the team and they have the know-how to do this job. Tasks related to database administration are adding, viewing, editing and deleting user records and breathing exercises. The goal of the database administrator is to organize all data and to make the developers jobs easier.

Features/Functional Requirements

Section 1 User

As a user, I want to:

1.     Account Features

a.     Create an account either from email or through Facebook/Google so that I can track in-app stats (not require a log in to use basic app features?)

b.     Switch between accounts so that I can use the app on the same device with others

c.     Share my results/points with friends and vice versa so that I can see others progress

d.     See my history on the app so that I can track my progress

e.     Opt out of sharing my usage data so that I can keep my information private

f.      Provide feedback on breathing exercises so that the developers can improve their app

g.     Leave comments on breathing exercises so that other users can know about my experience

2.     Resources

a.     Know why breathing exercises help so that I can justify using Breathify

b.     Access more resources on anxiety and stress so that I can find help if Breathify is not enough (finding help is the focus)

c.     Something about finding out more about stress and anxiety

3.     Menus

a.     Go through a tutorial so that I know how to use the app properly

b.     Use this app even without internet so that I can access these exercises anywhere

c.     Have access to multiple breathing exercises so that I can find one that works for me

d.     Sort exercises in categories so that I can choose one that is useful for me

e.     Leave feedback on exercises so that I can let other users know what I think is useful

f.      Read about exercises so I know how they can help me

4.     Timer based

a.     Use a simple breathing exercise so that I can de-stress

b.     Set reminders so that I can practice these exercises at certain times and intervals every day (ex. On the bus, after waking up, etc.)

c.     Pause the exercise so that I can come back to it later

d.     Restart the exercise in case I missed a part

e.     Mute the aural cues so that I can use the app silently

f.      Monitor my heart rate so that I can keep track of the effectiveness of the different exercises

5.     Game based

a.     Play games that relate to breathing so that I can enjoy doing something that helps me be productive

b.     Keep track of score so that I can have an incentive for following the exercise

c.     Make my own breathing exercise so that I can figure out what works for me

d.     Pause the exercise so that I can come back to it later

e.     Restart the exercise in case I missed a part

 

Section 2 Developer

As a developer, I want to:

1.     Have a clear idea of the look and feel of the app so that I can provide a consistent interface

2.     Know what information needs to be private so that I can encrypt it

3.     Create breathing exercises so that I can expand the variety of exercises

4.     Edit breathing exercises so that I can correct any mistakes in the exercise

 

Section 3 Database Administrator

As a database administrator, I want to:

1.     Create, maintain and delete breathing exercises so that I can continually update the app

2.     Create, add, remove, and update user profiles and user data so that I can maintain the database

 

Section 4 Tester

As a tester, I want to:

1.     Know the features and functions clearly so that I can develop validation tests

 

Section 5 Sponsor

As a sponsor, I want to:

1.     Recommend this app to clients/customers so that I can help them

2.     Have resources for users so that they can stay informed about anxiety and stress

3.     Have users be able to contact the appropriate people so that they can do so in an emergency

 

 

Non-functional Requirements

 

Section 1 Product Requirements

1.      Usability requirements

a.       The basic functions of the app (such as functions that are not required for a log in) must be accessible anytime.

b.      The breathing exercises must be able to stop by the user when in process.

c.       The app must provide clear instructions of the breathing exercises and the user must be able to skip or replay the instructions whenever they want to.

d.      The loading time to open the app must not take more than ~5 seconds

e.       The processing times of going through the menus and exercises must not be more than .5 seconds in order for the app to have a smooth performance

f.       The querying time to obtain data from the server should not take more than 2 seconds with good internet connection

g.      Users must be able to authenticate themselves using an e-mail/password combination or use Facebook/Google login authentication.

h.      Users must be able to request a password reset.

2.      Localization requirements

a.       The app and help instructions must be released in English.

3.      Performance requirements

a.       The breathing exercise must save at the "breath in" state when users go to the home page of IOS with the app still running in the background. When this happens, the system must still keep record each breath period.

b.      The user device must require iOS 8.0 or later. Compatible with iPhone (4/4.7/5.5 inch) and iPad

c.       The appearance of the app must be attractive. The design should promote a positive and relaxed experience for the user.

4.      Dependency requirements

a.       Obtaining heart rate information must have permission from the user to use device camera.

5.      Reliability requirements

a.       The probability that the app will execute without failure must be greater than 99%.

b.      The database must always be accessible given an internet connection.

c.       The database must be able to handle a lot of traffic, approximately < 5000 requests a day

6.      Security requirements

a.       The app must not use or transmit personal data without obtaining permission from the user. If the user gives permission, the user must be informed by how and where their data will be used.

b.      Personal information must not be accessible to unauthorized users.

c.       All private data must be encrypted when being transmitted between database and user.

Section 2 Organizational Requirements

1.      Environmental requirements

a.       iOS

2.      Operational requirements

a.       Programming language: Swift

b.      The app must only use public APIs as set out in the App Store Guidelines 2.5.1.

3.      Development requirements

a.       The data (breath record) must be saved locally first if the user doesnt have an account. Once the user logs in, the user is able to upload the local data or be given the option to replace local data with the most updated data from the server.

b.      Data from multiple accounts can be stored locally at once.

                 a.           In case of users that will opt out from uploading their exercise log data into the database, the user's information and log data must remain locally in the device.

                 b.           Local data will only be lost when the app is deleted. In order to save the local data, it must be uploaded to the database.

Section 3 External Requirements

1.      Regulatory requirements

a.       The system must have functions that aim to improve the breathing, stress and anxiety.

2.      Ethical requirements

a.       The app must inform the user that the 'Breathify' is not a medical application and that it should not be used solely to treat medical/mental illnesses.

b.      A section in the app must contain accurate information and resources about anxiety, stress and breathing.

3.      Safety requirements

a.       The app must not cause harm to the user, such as give unsafe timings during breathing exercises. Since users would want to use this app to relax, there should be no inaccurate information to detriment the health of the user.

b.      There must be no offensive or insensitive content within the app.

c.       There must be a privacy policy to inform the user about the security and the use of the data obtained in the app.
